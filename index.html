The Guiding Light: The Life of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH)

  1. Early Life and Childhood of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH)
    • Born in 570 CE in Mecca, part of the Arabian Peninsula
    • Born to Amina bint Wahb and Abdullah ibn Abd al-Muttalib
    • His father passed away before his birth, and his mother passed away when he was six years old
    • Raised by his grandfather, Abdul-Muttalib, and later by his uncle, Abu Talib
    • Known for his honesty and integrity from a young age, earning the nickname Al-Amin (the trustworthy)
  2. Adolescent Years of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH)
    • Worked as a shepherd to help his family
    • Known for his truthful and trustworthy nature among his peers
    • Gained experience in trade and business by traveling with trade caravans
    • Exhibited wisdom and fairness, which earned him respect in the community
  3. The Revelation and the Beginning of Prophethood
    • At the age of 40, while meditating in the Cave of Hira, he received the first revelation from Angel Gabriel
    • The revelation marked the beginning of his mission as the Prophet of Islam
    • Faced initial resistance and hostility from the Quraysh tribe
    • Gradually gained followers who believed in the message of Islam
    • Emphasized the worship of one God (Allah), social justice, and moral conduct
  4. The Night Journey and Ascension (Isra and Mi'raj)
    • Occurred around 621 CE, a significant event in Islamic tradition
    • Isra: The night journey from Mecca to Jerusalem on the Buraq (a heavenly steed)
    • Mi'raj: The ascension to the heavens, where he met various prophets and was granted the command of five daily prayers
    • This event is celebrated annually on the 27th of Rajab in the Islamic calendar
  5. The Final Years and the Legacy of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH)
    • Migrated to Medina in 622 CE, an event known as the Hijra, marking the beginning of the Islamic calendar
    • Established the first Muslim community and state in Medina
    • Engaged in several battles to defend the Muslim community and spread Islam
    • Delivered the Farewell Sermon during his final pilgrimage, emphasizing equality, justice, and piety
    • Passed away in 632 CE in Medina, leaving a profound and lasting impact on the world
